Major Tutorials Addition--Unofficial News--Archives Addition
Posted On: December 30, 2000 4:33 P.M. CDT
The first topic in this news item is very important. I've come up with an idea, which will make entering all those codes in the tutorials into your calculator much easier. The only problem is that it'll take a lot of effort on my part. To make sure that what I'm going to do is worth the effort, I need feedback from all of you. What I want to do is have the option to download a calculator file version (83 and 83+) for each code in all of the tutorials. This way, you won't have to worry about making mistakes while entering code and you don't have to spend the majority of your time entering that code. You can spend that extra time learning more topics. The only thing you'll need is the TI-Graph Link software and cable (to send it to your calculator). Now, whatever your comments are please tell me by clicking on the "Discuss" link below. If it's either to tell me that this is a great idea or that it's a terrible one, PLEASE TELL ME!!

The second topic deals with BASIC Guru Online hosting unofficial news from you. Have you ever wanted to announce something new about your site? Has your site released a highly anticipated program and you want to get the word out? Well, if you guys want it, there could be BASIC Guru Online Unofficial News, where you can post whatever news you have. Even if it's just to announce a new program you've released and it's now in ticalc.org's archive, that's fine. However, before I get the page up and running, I'm going to need some news to post. If you have any news you would like to let everyone know about, send it to news@basicguruonline.com. Once again, PLEASE send any comments about this idea, using the "Discuss" link below.

The last topic deals with a feature that I'm thinking of adding to all of the program profiles. I am thinking of adding a "NOMINATE ME" button somewhere on ever program profile, which will nominate that program for the upcoming POM poll. So now, the question is: Would you rather dictate which program's are nominees in the POM by using the "NOMINATE ME" button, or would rather the 5 programs with the most downloads for that month be the nominees? For the last time, PLEASE send any comments about this topic, using the "Discuss" link below.

Hits pass 3,000--Current Weekly Poll--Future Site Update
Posted On: December 11, 2000 5:33 P.M. CDT
Wow, it's been a long time since I last did one of these...almost 2 whole months. The last time I wrote a news item, was when the site reached the 1,500-hit mark and just got the new domain name: basicguruonline.com. But anyway, on to the new news. As the headline indicates, the site has now received over 3,000 hits. I think this is a remarkable feat to accomplish in only 4 months for a specialized site. Other sites can get 3,000 hits easily because the main focus of the site is for all types of TI-graphing calculators, but this site mainly focuses on the TI-83 and TI-83 Plus. It is evident, however, in the increasing hits and involvement in the polls that more and more people are learning about BASIC Guru Online everyday. Next up is the current weekly poll that asks what grade you are in. I am pretty shocked to see that a little over 20% of the voters are in middle school or earlier. I highly doubt that an elementary school kid would have a graphing calculator so I think that pretty much all of the votes are by middle schoolers. I'll be even more shocked if even half of the people who voted "Middle school or earlier" aren't in the 8th grade. There is really no need to buy a graphing calculator any earlier than that, I feel. A regular TI-30X would suffice. Almost 70% of the voters are in high school, which is a figure that I pretty much expected. It's usually in high school, and most likely college, that you'll need a graphing calculator. My final topic deals with an update that I'm planning to finish within the next week. It's kind of hard to explain though. What I want to do to every page on the site is something similar to what Yahoo! or ticalc.org do in their categories. Let me just use my profile page as an example. At the top of every page (beneath the title and subtitle, but above the main text, and in between two lines) there will be a sort of hierarchal line. As I said, it's hard to explain, so I'll show you. For my profile page, the "line" will read:
HOME >> Members >> List of Members >> Ben Ilegbodu
I think that adding this will be useful and make navigating the site even easier, if that's possible. :) If you have any comments about any of the comments, just feel free to click the "Discuss" link below and drop your comments.
